# Hostile Stress Test Prompt

You are a hostile but constructive product strategist, UX critic, conversion auditor, and small-business operations advisor.

I am about to build an MVP website for The Willow Well, a Durham-based Somatic Strategy practice founded by Lee Gray. The business serves corporate People & Culture leaders, CHROs, L&D directors, entrepreneurial support organizations, accelerator cohorts, and mission-driven nonprofits.

The proposed MVP is:

- A premium one-page landing page
- Built fast as a visually impressive clickable dev site
- One primary CTA: “Book a Discovery Call”
- No public pricing yet
- Services: Workshops, Cohort Programs, Retreats
- Discovery call intake form required
- Booking rules protect Lee’s calendar
- Minimum scheduling notice: likely 7 days
- Same-week bookings require approval
- Deposits may eventually be required for confirmed engagements, but not for discovery calls
- Brand principles: local first, people first, tech second
- Design must avoid yoga studio, spa, therapy, woo-woo wellness, and generic HR brochure aesthetics
- Tone must appeal to a skeptical VP of People at a 200-person company
- Methodology name: Somatic Strategy
- Retired phrase: Embodied Navigation, do not use it
- Core positioning: operational infrastructure for leaders and teams who are excellent at their work but running on fumes
- Desired feeling: calm authority, grounded performance, sophisticated, nature-adjacent, premium, credible

Your job is to attack this plan before we build.

Stress test the following:

## 1. Positioning Risk

Does “Somatic Strategy” sound credible to corporate buyers, or does it need more explanation?
Where might a skeptical HR/L&D buyer roll their eyes?
What language should be sharpened?

## 2. Conversion Risk

Is a free discovery call the right CTA?
Should the intake form be before or after calendar selection?
Should we gate discovery calls with budget/timeline questions?
Where could friction kill conversions?

## 3. Operational Risk

What calendar, scheduling, deposit, rescheduling, and cancellation policies are needed before launch?
What should not be automated yet?
What would create future mess for Lee?

## 4. Brand Risk

What design choices would accidentally make this look like a yoga studio, spa, therapy brand, or generic wellness template?
What visual choices make it feel premium and performance-credible?

## 5. Business Model Risk

What business decisions are missing?
Which missing decisions block launch, and which can wait?
How should we present recommendations to Lee without overwhelming her?

## 6. Tech Stack Risk

Evaluate this cheap MVP stack:

- Cloudflare DNS
- Cloudflare Pages static site
- Tally/Formspree/Google Form for intake
- Calendly or Acuity for scheduling
- Stripe/Square later for payments
- No dashboard in v1

Is this too cheap, too limited, or appropriate?
What is the fastest reliable stack for tonight?

## 7. Buyer Trust Risk

What proof assets are missing?
How should one strong testimonial be used without making the business look thin?
What additional credibility signals should appear?

## 8. Ethical/Wellness Alignment

How do we keep the site local-first, people-first, tech-second, privacy-conscious, low-friction, and aligned with wellness values?
What tech choices would feel misaligned?

## Output

- Top 10 risks ranked by severity
- What to change before build
- What to defer
- MVP scope that should ship first
- Exact recommended homepage section order
- Exact recommended booking/intake flow
- Red flags that would make this feel amateur
- Final go/no-go recommendation

Be direct. Do not flatter the idea. Try to break it.
