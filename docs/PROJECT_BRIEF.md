# The Willow Well Project Brief

## Business

The Willow Well is a somatic wellness and leadership practice founded by Lee Gray. It serves corporate teams, entrepreneurial support organizations, accelerator cohorts, and nonprofits.

The practice combines nervous system regulation with strategic clarity work to help high-performing leaders and teams build sustainable capacity.

## Important Positioning

This is not a yoga studio.
This is not an executive coaching firm.
This is not a traditional corporate wellness vendor.

The Willow Well occupies its own category: operational infrastructure for leaders and teams who are excellent at their work but running on fumes.

## Methodology

Use: Somatic Strategy

Do not use: Embodied Navigation

Somatic Strategy is the practice of building organizational performance capacity through the body by regulating the nervous system so leaders and teams can think clearly, decide confidently, and sustain output without burning out.

## Somatic Strategy Arc

01 Arrive  
Regulate the room. Nervous system first before any agenda item.

02 Move  
Somatic practice that shifts state through breath, body, and intentional movement.

03 Excavate  
Strategic clarity work around values, patterns, and what is driving behavior.

04 Commit  
Leave with a real tool and a real intention, not inspiration that evaporates.

## Services

### Workshops

Half-day and full-day intensives designed for corporate teams, accelerator cohorts, and organizational off-sites. Every session follows the Somatic Strategy arc and delivers a tool participants take back to work the same day. Custom-scoped for your context.

### Cohort Programs

Ongoing programs that build capacity over time. The Rooted Practice at Kate's Korner Learning Center is the model: a six-month staff wellness cohort that meets twice weekly and delivers measurable behavioral change. Built for organizations ready to invest in their people beyond a single event.

### Retreats

Half-day to two-day immersive experiences for teams ready to do the deeper work. Breathwork, somatic movement, values clarification, strategic wayfinding, closing ceremony. These are the engagements teams mark time by.
