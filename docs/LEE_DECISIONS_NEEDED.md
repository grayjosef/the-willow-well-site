# Decisions Needed From Lee

This site can launch visually before every business rule is finalized, but the following decisions will make the business easier to run, more profitable, and less stressful.

## 1. Good-Fit Client

What makes someone a strong fit right now?

Examples:
- Corporate team
- Founder cohort
- Accelerator program
- Mission-driven nonprofit
- Leadership team
- Staff wellness cohort

Why this matters:
It helps the site filter for opportunities worth your time, energy, and preparation.

## 2. Discovery Call Availability

What days and times should discovery calls be available?

Why this matters:
This protects your week and prevents last-minute calendar stress.

## 3. Minimum Notice

How much notice do you need before a client-facing session?

Recommended default:
7 days.

Why this matters:
The work is better when you have time to prepare.

## 4. Weekly Cutoff

Should new bookings for the coming week close after a certain point?

Recommended default:
Sunday at 5:00 PM.

Why this matters:
This lets you review your week before it starts and prevents surprise appointments.

## 5. Service Price Ranges

What are the rough internal price ranges for:

- Workshops
- Cohort programs
- Retreats

Why this matters:
Prices do not need to be public, but internal ranges help you respond confidently after discovery calls.

## 6. Deposit Policy

Should confirmed engagement dates require a deposit?

Recommended starting point:
10 percent non-refundable deposit for confirmed engagements.

Why this matters:
A deposit protects your calendar and signals that the client is serious.

## 7. Rescheduling Policy

How many times can a client reschedule?

Recommended starting point:
One reschedule with at least seven days’ notice.

Why this matters:
This protects your time while still being reasonable.

## 8. Cancellation Policy

What happens if a client cancels close to the engagement date?

Why this matters:
Calendar holds have real cost.

## 9. Travel Policy

What travel radius is included?
What travel costs are billed to the client?

Why this matters:
“Available for travel” should not mean unpaid logistics.

## 10. Proof Assets

What can we use?

- Testimonials
- Client logos
- Session photos
- Retreat photos
- Cohort examples
- Measurable outcomes
- Referral quotes

Why this matters:
Proof makes the site feel established and premium.
